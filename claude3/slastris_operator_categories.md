# Slastris — Operator Parameter Categories (First Draft)

## Framing

Each category describes a **mode of world participation** — not a stat to increment,
but a dimension along which entities, objects, and world tendencies differ.
Low and high are not "worse" and "better". They are genuinely different
ways of existing in the Packet world.

---

## 1. FLOW — Continuity

**What it governs:**
How naturally an entity sustains transformation between states.
High Continuity means motion and change flow without resistance.
Low Continuity means each transition requires effort or breaks down.

**Low:** entity moves in discrete jumps, struggles with sustained motion,
loses coherence when changing state rapidly.

**High:** entity transitions seamlessly between states, can sustain long
chains of transformation without entropy cost.

**Across:**
- Agent: how smoothly it traverses operator zones without friction
- Object: whether it can be moved/transformed without breaking
- World gen: how continuous or jagged zone boundaries form; smooth fields vs sharp seams

---

## 2. CONNECT — Relational Reach

**What it governs:**
How many simultaneous relations an entity can hold or exploit.
Not how it moves, but how far its participation extends into the surrounding field.

**Low:** entity exists in isolation, unaffected by and unable to affect
relations between adjacent cells or other entities.

**High:** entity actively binds to surrounding operator structure, creating
chains of dependency; its presence modifies nearby field behavior.

**Across:**
- Agent: range and depth of awareness of other agents and objects
- Object: whether it passively occupies space or actively binds to field
- World gen: density of connection between zone types; how entangled masses are

---

## 3. DISSOLVE — Release Threshold

**What it governs:**
How readily an entity surrenders form under pressure.
This is not weakness — it is the willingness and speed of structural release.

**Low:** entity holds form rigidly even under incompatible operator pressure;
resists dissolution but accumulates internal stress.

**High:** entity releases form quickly when field conditions shift;
low stress accumulation, but identity becomes diffuse under sustained pressure.

**Across:**
- Agent: how quickly it collapses or transforms when entering hostile zones
- Object: fragility / destructibility; how fast it becomes wall or residue
- World gen: how aggressively TURBULENCE zones dissolve into other forms; seam behavior

---

## 4. ENCODE — Pattern Retention

**What it governs:**
How much structural information an entity preserves across state changes.
High Encode = memory-like; entity carries history of its transformations.
Low Encode = stateless; each new state is independent of the last.

**Low:** entity treats each moment as fresh; no accumulated pattern,
no learned behavior, no signature left in the field.

**High:** entity accumulates pattern; its presence leaves recognizable traces,
its behavior is shaped by prior states, it can recognize repeated configurations.

**Across:**
- Agent: behavioral memory; whether it adapts to repeated encounters
- Object: whether it retains modification history (worn, marked, charged)
- World gen: how much CRYSTAL retains the memory of prior regen cycles; geological-like layering

---

## 5. CHOOSE — Decision Pressure

**What it governs:**
How forcefully an entity collapses possibility into action.
Not intelligence — raw decisiveness. The speed and finality of path selection.

**Low:** entity hesitates at branch points, occupies multiple possible paths
simultaneously for longer, acts with low commitment.

**High:** entity collapses possibilities immediately and fully; commits to
one path and executes without residual uncertainty; harder to redirect.

**Across:**
- Agent: reaction speed to branching field conditions; commit depth
- Object: whether it can occupy ambiguous states or must resolve quickly
- World gen: how sharply zone boundaries form; whether transition regions are wide or knife-edge

---

## 6. OBSERVE — Perceptual Depth

**What it governs:**
How far and how accurately an entity reads the Packet world around it.
Not range alone — depth of reading. Can it detect TURBULENCE, or only MANIFEST?

**Low:** entity sees only immediate adjacency; cannot detect hidden layers;
responds only to what is directly manifest.

**High:** entity reads turbulence pressure, latent structure, crystal proximity;
acts on field truth beyond the visible layer; harder to surprise.

**Across:**
- Agent: effective awareness radius and layer depth (can it sense LATENT cells? TURBULENCE?)
- Object: whether it emits detectable signals into the field or stays invisible
- World gen: how much the generation algorithm "looks ahead" — local vs long-range zone influence

---

## 7. CYCLE — Iteration Depth

**What it governs:**
How many times an entity can repeat a process and still extract meaningful change.
Low Cycle entities exhaust quickly. High Cycle entities sustain recursive behavior
without diminishing returns.

**Low:** entity performs an action once, cannot repeat productively;
subsequent repetitions produce noise or no change.

**High:** entity gains from repetition; repeated passes through the same
zone deepen its effect; recursive strategies are available to it.

**Across:**
- Agent: whether repeated traversal of the same field region accumulates effect
- Object: charge-up behavior; whether repeated interaction changes it
- World gen: how many regen cycles a region survives before crystallizing vs dissolving

---

## 8. LOGIC — Constraint Binding

**What it governs:**
How strongly an entity is governed by field rules vs how freely it can violate them.
High Logic = strictly rule-bound. Low Logic = anomalous, partially outside normal operator law.

**Low:** entity partially ignores field operator constraints; can exist in
incompatible zones without full pressure; behaves anomalously.

**High:** entity strictly follows operator compatibility rules; gains efficiency
inside compatible zones; cannot survive or function in incompatible space.

**Across:**
- Agent: whether it obeys zone boundaries or can cross them; how much density affects it
- Object: whether it follows normal field physics or has anomalous properties
- World gen: strictness of operator compatibility enforcement; how much TURBULENCE is tolerated

---

## 9. RUNTIME — State Coherence

**What it governs:**
How stable an entity's internal state is across time and field variation.
Not whether it changes — whether its identity persists through change.

**Low:** entity's state fluctuates with every field change; it is highly
reactive, easy to destabilize, has no stable baseline.

**High:** entity maintains coherent internal state across turbulent conditions;
field pressure accumulates slowly; identity persists through zone transitions.

**Across:**
- Agent: resistance to accumulated field pressure; how long it stays effective
- Object: durability of function; whether it degrades or holds form under pressure
- World gen: stability of the base field between regen cycles; how much drift occurs

---

## 10. MANIFEST — Expression Strength

**What it governs:**
How strongly an entity's presence registers in the field — how much it
actually changes what is around it vs passing through without effect.

**Low:** entity passes through field leaving no trace; low impact on density,
operator distribution, or turbulence; existentially quiet.

**High:** entity actively reshapes local field as it moves; its presence
biases density, operator dominance, and manifestation in its wake.

**Across:**
- Agent: how much its movement modifies the local field; territorial pressure
- Object: whether it anchors local operator structure or is neutral to it
- World gen: how strongly zone seeds express outward; seeding vs quiet background generation

---

## Notes

These categories are not independent sliders.
Some will interact meaningfully:
- High OBSERVE + Low CHOOSE = entity reads much but acts slowly
- High MANIFEST + Low RUNTIME = entity reshapes field but cannot sustain itself
- High DISSOLVE + High CYCLE = entity collapses and re-forms repeatedly

Those interactions are not designed here — they will emerge from the substrate.
Do not force them. Let Packet world reveal which combinations are stable.
